    A little bit about me.

    My name is Bettsie Flores and this page is be about me. My major is in computer information systems, and I intend to graduate in spring of 2019. In those three years, the classes I took were python, Principles of programming, computer graphics, Object-oriented programming. The classes I finished taking are computer repair, support 1, support 2, and web development. On my first year at Carroll, I majored in surgical technology. The unique reason why I decided switched my major was because of time and money. To be a surgeon it is another 12 years of school and a lot of money. So, I researched for weeks trying to find an associates degree career, I wanted a major where I could learn something new and a major that had job growth. That is how I decided to go with computer information systems. Now, The next few panels are based on what I enjoy in my life.

  • Motorcycles

    I enjoy riding.

    I have always had a passion for motorcycles, well anything with two wheels. When I saw, the motorcycles classes happen on Carroll Community College, that is when I knew I wanted to ride a motorcycle. Of course, when I mentioned it to my mother, she told me I was crazy and hoped it was just a phase. I also mentioned it to some of my closest friends, and they thought I was crazy as well. It took a lot of convincing, but I did get my first motorcycle. I also took motorcycle classes at Carroll and got my license for it. Now, I have been riding for a year and a half. I love riding. I feel relaxed and happy when I get to ride. The only problem is that I do not know anyone who shares the same passion.

  • Animals

    I am an animal lover.

    I have always had a passion for animals. When I was younger, I had an orange tabby, but unfortunately it tried to escape out an open window. When it tried to escape, it got stuck and died. From there my mom owned seven rescued dogs. Though, we had to give them away because we were moving to Maryland. I missed having companions, so eventually I rescued a kitten that was thrown out by a family. When I got to keep him, I realized he had been abused and was in bad shape. Now, he is my best friend, and he has been with my for ten years. He is the cat with the green hat. I thought he was going to be the only companion, but when we moved two years ago, I crossed paths with another cat. This cat just showed up at our door and we were not going to keep her, but our neighbors wanted to give her away to the humane society. The only bad thing was that she was pregnant, and the humane society wanted to kill them. I did not want that for her, so I gave her shelter until she gave birth to the kittens. My plan was to give them away to different families; however, my whole family fell in love with the five kittens she had. Yes, five kittens, I was surprised because I expected her to have one or two. The kittens recently turned one this October, one week before my birthday. The best gift I could ask for. Of course, the whole process of having six more cats is hard, but I was glad to cross paths with a lady I met. The lady I met rescues cats and dogs along with her husband. Without her I would not have kept those cats. Either way, I love each and every single one of my cats.
